Understanding the Graphical Method

The Graphical Method solves Linear Programming problems with two decision variables by visualizing constraints and finding the optimal solution geometrically.

Key Concepts
  • Feasible Region: The area satisfying all constraints simultaneously
  • Corner Points: Vertices of the feasible region
  • Optimal Solution: Always occurs at a corner point (or along an edge)
Corner Point Method
1. Graph all constraints as lines 2. Identify the feasible region 3. Find all corner points (intersections) 4. Evaluate Z at each corner point 5. Select max (or min) Z value
Special Cases
  • Unbounded: Feasible region extends infinitely in the optimization direction
  • Infeasible: No region satisfies all constraints
  • Multiple Optima: Objective function parallel to a constraint edge
Iso-profit Lines

Lines where Z = constant. Move the iso-profit line in the direction of optimization until it leaves the feasible region—the last corner point touched is optimal.
