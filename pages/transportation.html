Understanding the Transportation Problem

The Transportation Problem minimizes the cost of shipping goods from multiple sources to multiple destinations while satisfying supply and demand constraints.

Initial Basic Feasible Solution (IBFS) Methods
  • North-West Corner: Start from top-left, allocate maximum possible, move right or down
  • Least Cost Method: Allocate to the cell with minimum cost first
  • Vogel's Approximation (VAM): Calculate penalty (difference between two smallest costs), allocate to highest penalty
MODI (Modified Distribution) Optimization
For basic cells: uᵢ + vⱼ = cᵢⱼ

Opportunity cost for non-basic cells: Δᵢⱼ = cᵢⱼ - uᵢ - vⱼ

If any Δᵢⱼ < 0, solution is not optimal
Loop Construction
  • Enter the most negative Δᵢⱼ cell
  • Form a closed loop using basic cells
  • Alternate + and - signs starting with + at entering cell
  • Transfer minimum of - cells to + cells
Total Cost Calculation

Total Cost = Σ (allocation × unit cost) for all allocated cells
